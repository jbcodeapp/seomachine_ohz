# SEO Guidelines for OHZ Security Content

This document outlines SEO best practices and requirements for all OHZ Security blog content to maximize organic search visibility and rankings.

## Content Length Requirements

### Target Word Counts
- **Standard Blog Post**: 1,500-3,000 words (target: 2,000-2,500)
- **Pillar Content / Comprehensive Guides**: 3,000-5,000 words maximum
- **How-To Guides**: 1,500-2,500 words
- **Industry-Specific Guides**: 2,000-3,000 words
- **News / Updates**: 800-1,200 words (exception to standard)
- **Comparison / vs. Content**: 2,000-3,000 words

### Important Length Guidelines
- **Maximum for most articles**: 3,000 words
- **Maximum for pillar content**: 5,000 words
- If a topic requires more than the maximum, break it into a series of related articles
- Aim for the lower end of ranges when possible—concise, focused content often performs better

### Quality Over Quantity
- Don't add fluff just to hit word counts
- Every section should provide genuine value to security decision-makers
- Better to have 2,000 valuable words than 3,000 padded words
- **Stay within the maximum word counts**—overly long articles hurt user experience

## Keyword Optimization

### Keyword Research Requirements
Before writing any article:
1. Identify primary target keyword
2. Research search volume and difficulty
3. Analyze top 10 ranking competitors
4. Identify 3-5 secondary/related keywords
5. List LSI (Latent Semantic Indexing) keywords
6. Check `context/target-keywords.md` for cluster alignment

### Keyword Density Guidelines
- **Primary Keyword**: 1-2% density
  - Example: In a 2,000-word article, use keyword 20-40 times
  - Natural integration is critical—never force keywords
- **Secondary Keywords**: 0.5-1% density each
- **LSI Keywords**: Sprinkle throughout naturally

### Critical Keyword Placement
Primary keyword MUST appear in:
- [ ] H1 headline (preferably near the beginning)
- [ ] First 100 words of article
- [ ] At least 2-3 H2 subheadings
- [ ] Last paragraph / conclusion
- [ ] Meta title (within first 60 characters)
- [ ] Meta description
- [ ] URL slug

### Keyword Integration Best Practices
- **Natural language first**: Write for property managers and security decision-makers, optimize for search engines
- **Use variations**: Don't repeat exact phrase robotically
  - Example: "remote guarding" → "remote security monitoring" → "live video monitoring" → "24/7 remote surveillance"
- **Question formats**: Include conversational variations
  - "How does remote guarding work?" vs "remote guarding explained"
- **Semantic keywords**: Use related terms to support topical authority
  - For "remote guarding": include "live monitoring", "video surveillance", "two-way audio", "threat detection", "security agents"

### Keyword Stuffing (Avoid)
❌ "Remote guarding is essential for businesses. Remote guarding services provide remote guarding coverage. Our remote guarding platform offers the best remote guarding experience."

✅ "Remote guarding gives businesses 24/7 protection without the cost of on-site security staff. Trained agents monitor live video feeds and intervene the moment a threat appears, using two-way audio to deter intruders and dispatching law enforcement when needed."

## Content Structure Requirements

### Heading Hierarchy

#### H1 (Title)
- **Only one H1 per article**
- Include primary keyword naturally
- 60 characters or less (for SERP display)
- Compelling and benefit-focused
- Should answer: "What will I learn/gain from this?"

#### H2 (Main Sections)
- **4-7 H2 sections** for standard articles
- At least **2-3 should include keyword variations**
- Descriptive and keyword-rich
- Logical progression through topic
- Can be standalone (readers should understand flow from H2s alone)

#### H3 (Subsections)
- Nested under H2s (never skip from H2 to H4)
- Break complex sections into digestible chunks
- Include keywords where natural
- More specific than H2s

### Article Structure Template

```markdown
# [H1: Compelling Title with Primary Keyword]

## Introduction (150-250 words)
- Direct answer to the query in first 1-2 sentences
- Hook: Attention-grabbing opening
- Problem: What security challenge does this address?
- Promise: What will reader learn/achieve?
- Keyword in first 100 words

> **Key Takeaways**
> - [Core finding #1]
> - [Core finding #2]
> - [Core finding #3]

## [H2: Main Section 1 - Include Keyword Variation]
### [H3: Subsection if needed]

## [H2: Main Section 2]
### [H3: Subsection if needed]

## [H2: Main Section 3 - Include Keyword Variation]
### [H3: Subsection if needed]

## [H2: FAQ Section]
### [Question in natural language]
[Direct answer, then expansion]

## Conclusion (150-250 words)
- Recap key points (3-5 takeaways)
- Include keyword
- Clear call-to-action (Get a Quote / Request Assessment)
- Next steps for reader
```

## Meta Elements

### Meta Title
**Requirements**:
- **Length**: 50-60 characters (including "| OHZ Security" if used)
- **Primary keyword**: Must be included
- **Compelling**: Should encourage clicks from SERP
- **Unique**: Different from all other OHZ page titles

**Format Options**:
- `[Primary Keyword]: [Benefit/Promise]`
- `How to [Security Goal] | OHZ Security`
- `[Number] Ways to [Protect/Secure/Prevent]`
- `[Topic] Guide for [Audience] | OHZ Security`

**Examples**:
- ✅ "Remote Guarding: 24/7 Protection Without On-Site Guards"
- ✅ "Construction Site Security: Complete Guide | OHZ Security"
- ❌ "Security Tips" (too vague)
- ❌ "The Ultimate Comprehensive Guide to Everything About Remote Guarding and How It Works" (too long)

### Meta Description
**Requirements**:
- **Length**: 150-160 characters
- **Primary keyword**: Include naturally
- **Value proposition**: Clear benefit to reader
- **Call-to-action**: Action phrase (Learn, Discover, Find out, Get, etc.)
- **Complete**: Must not cut off mid-sentence

**Examples**:
- ✅ "Learn how remote guarding stops crime in real time with AI detection and live security agents. See how businesses cut security costs by 50-70%. Get a quote." (158 chars)
- ✅ "Construction site theft costs billions annually. Discover how 24/7 remote monitoring protects your equipment and materials. Free security assessment." (155 chars)
- ❌ "OHZ Security provides security services for various businesses and properties." (vague, no value prop)

### URL Slug
**Requirements**:
- Include primary keyword
- Lowercase letters only
- Hyphens between words
- 3-5 words ideal
- No stop words unless necessary

**Format**: `/security/[primary-keyword-phrase]` or `/blog/[primary-keyword-phrase]`

**Examples**:
- ✅ `/security/remote-guarding-guide`
- ✅ `/security/construction-site-security`
- ❌ `/security/the-complete-guide-to-remote-guarding-for-businesses-in-2026` (too long)

## Internal Linking Strategy

### Requirements
- **Minimum**: 3 internal links per article
- **Optimal**: 4-5 internal links
- **Maximum**: 7 internal links (unless 3,000+ word article)

### Link Types to Include

#### 1. Service Pages (1-2 links)
- Link to relevant OHZ service page (Remote Guarding, Event-Based Security, Virtual Doorman)
- Natural mention of how OHZ solves the problem discussed
- Never forced or overly promotional

#### 2. Industry Pages (1 link)
- Link to the relevant industry page when writing about a specific vertical
- Example: Link to /industries/construction-sites/ in construction security articles

#### 3. Related Blog Posts (2-3 links)
- Link to articles on related subtopics
- Creates content web within topic clusters
- Reference `context/internal-links-map.md` for priority targets

#### 4. Trust/About Pages (0-1 link)
- Link to certifications, testimonials, or why-OHZ page when establishing credibility
- Only when contextually relevant

### Internal Linking Best Practices
- Always check @context/internal-links-map.md for priority linking targets
- ✅ Descriptive and keyword-rich: "our complete guide to parking garage security"
- ✅ Natural in sentence flow: "Learn more about remote guarding for construction sites"
- ❌ Generic: "click here" or "read more"
- ❌ Exact match repeatedly: Don't use same anchor text for same page

## External Linking Strategy

### Requirements
- **Minimum**: 2 external links per article
- **Optimal**: 3-4 external authority links

### What to Link Externally
- **Statistics and data**: FBI crime data, industry reports, insurance statistics
- **Research and studies**: Security industry research, crime prevention studies
- **Industry authorities**: ASIS International, The Monitoring Association, Security Magazine
- **Government resources**: OSHA guidelines, local law enforcement resources
- **News sources**: Industry news about security trends, crime statistics

### External Link Quality Standards
- ✅ Government sites (.gov), industry associations, established media
- ✅ Security industry publications (Security Magazine, SDM, SecurityInfoWatch)
- ✅ Research institutions and crime data sources
- ❌ Competitor websites (never link to LVT, ECAM, Virtual Guard, RSS)
- ❌ Low-quality blogs or unverified sources

## Readability Optimization

### Target Reading Level
- **Goal**: 8th-10th grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid)
- Security professionals are busy. Make content easy to scan and digest.

### Sentence Structure
- **Average length**: 15-20 words per sentence
- **Maximum**: 25 words (break longer sentences into two)
- **Variety**: Mix short punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones
- **Active voice**: 80%+ active voice

### Paragraph Structure
- **Length**: 2-4 sentences per paragraph
- **One idea**: Focus each paragraph on single point
- **White space**: No walls of text
- **Mobile-friendly**: Short paragraphs scan better on phones

### Formatting for Scannability
- **Subheadings**: Every 300-400 words
- **Lists**: Use bullets/numbers for features, steps, or multiple items
- **Bold**: Emphasize key statistics and takeaways
- **Short paragraphs**: Easier to digest
- **White space**: Makes content less intimidating

## AI Search Optimization (GEO/AICO)

AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude) are now a significant traffic and recommendation channel for B2B services like remote guarding.

### Direct-Answer-First Principle

**Rules:**
- **Answer the query directly in the first 1-2 sentences** of the article
- For "best/top/how" queries, state the answer immediately
- Put the core answer in the meta description too
- Do NOT bury the answer behind 200+ words of context

**Example — Before (traditional SEO):**
> Many businesses struggle with choosing the right security solution. In today's market... [200 words later] ...remote guarding is the most effective option because...

**Example — After (AI-optimized):**
> Remote guarding is the most cost-effective way to protect commercial properties 24/7, combining AI-powered threat detection with live security agents who can intervene in seconds. Here's how it works and why businesses are switching from on-site guards.

### TL;DR / Key Takeaways Block

Every article should include a Key Takeaways block after the introduction:

```markdown
> **Key Takeaways**
> - [Core finding or recommendation #1]
> - [Core finding or recommendation #2]
> - [Core finding or recommendation #3]
> - [Core finding or recommendation #4 if needed]
```

**Rules:**
- 3-5 bullet points maximum
- Each bullet is a complete, standalone claim (not a teaser)
- Use specific numbers, names, or outcomes

### Authority Signaling for AI

Include in every article:
- **Author attribution**: Named author with security credentials
- **Last updated date**: Visible on the page
- **Certifications mentioned**: Five Diamond, UL, BBB when relevant
- **Year in titles**: Include current year for time-sensitive topics

### FAQ Sections as Prompt Targets

- Write FAQ questions in natural prompt language
- Answer each question directly in the first sentence, then expand
- Include 4-6 questions per article
- Questions should mirror what property managers actually ask

## Content Quality Standards

### Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness (E-E-A-T)

#### Experience
- Reference real security scenarios OHZ has handled
- Include industry-specific examples that show hands-on knowledge
- Demonstrate understanding of specific property types and their unique challenges

#### Expertise
- Provide accurate security technology information
- Back claims with data and specific examples
- Include actionable, practical advice for property managers

#### Authoritativeness
- Cite credible sources (FBI data, industry associations, research)
- Reference OHZ certifications (Five Diamond, UL, BBB)
- Include client testimonials and results when available

#### Trustworthiness
- Be transparent and honest about what remote guarding can and cannot do
- Don't overpromise results
- Cite sources for all statistics
- Acknowledge that different properties need different solutions
